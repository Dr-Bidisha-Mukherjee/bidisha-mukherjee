High Pressure Experimentalist

About Me

I am a researcher in experimental condensed matter physics, specializing in the study of double perovskite oxides under extreme conditions. My research focuses on high-pressure experiments using diamond anvil cells, micro-Raman spectroscopy, synchrotron-based XRD measurements, and low-temperature experiments. I am currently pursuing my Ph.D. at the National Center for High Pressure Studies (NCHPS), IISER Kolkata, under the supervision of Prof. Goutam Dev Mukherjee.

Research

Current Project

My research involves investigating the high-pressure behavior of Ho2CoMnO6, a 3d-4f double perovskite oxide, through synchrotron XRD and micro-Raman spectroscopic measurements. We have observed distinct slope changes of Raman modes at 6 GPa and 16 GPa, suggesting possible electronic, spin state, or magnetic transitions. Currently, I am collaborating with ESRF, France, ID24 X-ray absorption beamline for high-pressure XAS experiments to investigate potential spin-state transitions.
